Pharmaceutical Analysis — Question Bank

1. (A) Multiple choice questions. Each question carries equal marks :
  1. Complexometric titration are useful for the determination of ___________.
    (a) Metal ions
    (b) Non metal ions
    (c) Acidic ions
    (d) All of the above
  2. The number of moles of solute per litre of solvent is known as ___________.
    (a) Normality
    (b) Molarity
    (c) Molality
    (d) Formality
  3. Solubility of sparingly soluble salt can be determined by ___________.
    (a) Polarography
    (b) Conductometry
    (c) Potentiometry
    (d) None of these
  4. The direct titration of standard solution of iodine is called ___________.
    (a) Iodometry
    (b) Iodimetry
    (c) Redox titration
    (d) Indirect iodimetry
  5. Which analytical method is based on weight of precipitate ?
    (a) Acid base titration
    (b) Gravimetry
    (c) Precipitation titration
    (d) Complexometric titration
  6. Potentiometry is a/an __________ method of analysis.
    (a) Qualitative
    (b) Chromatographic
    (c) Electrochemical
    (d) Spectroscopic
  7. In complexometric titration ligand and metal ion join together by ___________.
    (a) H - bonding
    (b) Co-ordinate covalent bond
    (c) Covalent bond
    (d) Ionic bond
  8. In polarography __________ electrode is used.
    (a) Platinum
    (b) Glass
    (c) Dropping mercury
    (d) All of the above
  9. Solution of known concentration is called ___________.
    (a) Primary standard
    (b) Titrant
    (c) Indicator
    (d) Titrand
  10. Diffusion current can be correlated with different conditions by __________.
    (a) Nernst equation
    (b) Bragg's equation
    (c) Ilkovic equation
    (d) Beer's equation
  11. Use of adsorption indicators is the characteristic feature of :
    (a) Volhard's Method
    (b) Mohr's Method
    (c) Kjeldahl Method
    (d) Fajan's Method
  12. PM indicators are also referred as :
    (a) Monochrome
    (b) Auxochrome
    (c) Metallochrome
    (d) Dichrome
  13. A linear relationship exists between pH of solution and :
    (a) Turbidity of solution
    (b) Colour of solution
    (c) Potential of solution
    (d) Normality of solution
  14. Solubility of sparingly soluble salt can be determined by :
    (a) Conductometry
    (b) Potentiometry
    (c) Gravimetry
    (d) Fluorometry
  15. 0.1 N NaOH can be standardised by using potassium hydrogen phthalate or :
    (a) Oxalic Acid
    (b) Acetic Acid
    (c) Formic Acid
    (d) Ammonia
